Mix holds through the holidays. In the mature drive-thru/destination stores last summer the split barely moved from term into the school holidays — cold drinks + milkshakes were ~37% of sales in term vs ~35% in holidays (milkshakes flat, cold easing ~2pp as the early-summer heat passed). The cold-drink peak is weather-led (Jun–Jul), not the school calendar; total volume rose ~5%. So £ grows across all categories with only a small holiday tilt. Capture % = the share of transactions that include the category (attach rate, recent weeks) — a truer read of penetration than £. Food capture here is just ~11%, well below the other new sites — a clear add-on opportunity.
⚠️ Wastage — improving, keep tightening
Olney's wastage has come down sharply — from a ~13% peak in April (classic new-site over-ordering) to ~4% now, recent 4-week average 3.8%. It's on the right track and well below where Attleborough sits. Holding to a 3% target leaves ~£600 of retail waste to claim over the quarter (~£270 at cost).
