How F1 works. Every store is audited unannounced through the week. Qualifying (Mon–Thu) is a lighter check — queue speed plus hospitality (Hello, Goodbye, How-Are-You, Working the Queue) — and sets the grid for the weekend. Race (Fri–Sun) is the full audit (adds tables to brand, cleared within 3 mins, section/break-cover plan, visible team, dishwasher area) with the start handicapped by the Qualifying result. Lower queue/lap seconds and higher championship points are better.
Qualifying trend — the ceiling is high, the floor is low
Audit (W/C)Queue (s)HospitalityQuali rank
Brilliant one week, off the pace the next. Olney has hit 3rd twice (27 Apr at 126s, 12 May at 146s) — proof the team can run a top-3 queue. But in between and since it's bounced to 17th, 21st, 23rd and 15th, including a 27 May write-off (queue average 806s, goodbyes missed entirely → hospitality 55%). Hospitality is otherwise a reliable 100%. The opportunity isn't a new skill — it's consistency: lock in the routine that produced the 126s weeks so the floor stops collapsing.

Championship points awarded F1-style (higher = better). Finishing position is the weekend race result; the grid start was handicapped by that week's Qualifying.
Race form is drifting down. The opening three weekends were mid-pack (P14, P17, P16) but the last five have slipped — P19, P16, P19, P19, and P21 (dead last) on 7 Jun — with championship points falling 7 → 4 → 2. The weekend race start is handicapped by Qualifying, so the bumpy quali weeks are feeding straight through. Fix the quali consistency and the race result follows.
vs the chains — queue average (lower is better)
The hospitality edge is won; the speed edge isn't yet. On its best week Olney (126s) is ~2× faster than Nero (254s) and Costa (345s) and scores 100% hospitality where the chains score 0% — that's the ceiling, and the hospitality gap is a genuine moat. But Olney's 9-week average queue is ~307s, right in Costa/Nero territory. So the team out-serves the chains every week but only out-runs them on the good weeks. The prize is a reliable sub-200s queue — that turns an occasional win into a permanent one.
