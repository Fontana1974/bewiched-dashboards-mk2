Method. For each item we take the average units sold on each day of the week (last 8 complete weeks) and add up the days a delivery must cover: Tue covers Tue–Wed (2 days), Thu covers Thu–Fri (2 days), Sat covers Sat–Sun–Mon (3 days). Longest run is 3 days (the Saturday order — Monday is day 3, the last day in date) — within the 3-day shelf life. We then add a 15% buffer and round up. The buffer is a balance: too small and you risk lunchtime stockouts, too large and you waste stock given the 3-day life. † = demand too sparse to forecast reliably (under 10/week) — treat these as a guide and adjust by eye.

How F1 works. Every store is audited unannounced through the week. Qualifying (Mon–Thu) is a lighter check — queue speed plus hospitality (Hello, Goodbye, How-Are-You, Working the Queue) — and sets the grid for the weekend. Race (Fri–Sun) is the full audit (adds tables to brand, cleared within 3 mins, section/break-cover plan, visible team, dishwasher area) with the start handicapped by the Qualifying result. Lower queue seconds and higher championship points are better.
→Focus this week: Qualifying is now estate-leading — P1 two weeks running; carry that hospitality into the weekend Race.
