Mix holds through the holidays. In the mature drive-thru/destination stores last summer the split barely moved from term into the school holidays — cold drinks + milkshakes were ~37% of sales in term vs ~35% in holidays (milkshakes flat, cold easing ~2 percentage points as the early-summer heat passed). The cold-drink peak is weather-led (Jun–Jul), not the school calendar; total volume rose ~5%. So £ grows across all categories with only a small holiday tilt. Capture % = the share of transactions that include the category (attach rate, recent weeks) — a truer read of penetration than £.

How F1 works. Every store is audited unannounced through the week. Qualifying (Mon–Thu) is a lighter check — queue speed plus hospitality (Hello, Goodbye, How-Are-You, Working the Queue) — and sets the grid for the weekend. Race (Fri–Sun) is the full audit (adds tables to brand, cleared within 3 mins, section/break-cover plan, visible team, dishwasher area) with the start handicapped by the Qualifying result. Lower queue seconds and higher championship points are better.
→Focus this week: Qualifying just jumped to P2 (queue 134s) — bottle that and bring it to the Race weekend.
Qualifying trend — a sharp recovery to P2
Audit (W/C)Queue avgHospitalityQuali rank
The Qualifying story is recovery. Through April–May hospitality was the drag — Hello and Goodbye repeatedly scoring low (ranks 15–22). The last two audits flipped it: 100% hospitality on 3 Jun and a P2 finish on 8 Jun with queue down to 134s. The welcome is clearly fixable here — the job is to make the last fortnight the new normal.
→Focus this week: hold the hospitality gains — keep Hello & Goodbye at 100% every Qualifying.
Race results — Fri–Sun full audit · the inconsistency to fix
Race (W/C)Total scoreChamp. ptsFinish
Race weekends are the weak spot — and it's queue speed. NDT swings between strong weekends (P4 on 17 Apr, P8 twice, queue ~145s) and poor ones (P15s and P20 on 6 Jun, queue 211–312s). The pattern tracks weekend queue times almost exactly: when the drive-thru flows it finishes top-8; when it backs up it drops to the tail. Peak-hour deployment and a queue-buster at the window is the lever.
→Focus this week: P20 last weekend on a 211s queue — staff the window for flow at Fri–Sun peaks.
