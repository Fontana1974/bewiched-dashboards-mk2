Wastage has more than doubled since launch — 2.5% → 6.1%, while sales stayed flat. This is a par-level / production-discipline issue at a new site that hasn't dialled in demand yet, not a sales problem. Target: pull back to 3% (still above estate-best) to recover ~£2.1k retail over the quarter.

How F1 works. Every store is audited unannounced through the week. Qualifying (Mon–Thu) is a lighter check — queue speed plus hospitality (Hello, Goodbye, How-Are-You, Working the Queue) — and sets the grid for the weekend. Race (Fri–Sun) is the full audit (adds tables to brand, cleared within 3 mins, section/break-cover plan, visible team, dishwasher area) with the start handicapped by the Qualifying result. Lower queue/lap seconds and higher championship points are better.

Attleborough's race form is the best of the new sites. It has climbed P14 → P11 → P13 → P4 → P2 → P2, on the podium the last two weekends, with championship points up to 22 and queue average falling to ~150s. This is a genuine win to call out in BrewCrew Kudos Hour and a template to share with Olney and Billing.
Qualifying trend — the gap is Hello at the door
Audit (W/C)Queue avgHospitalityQuali rank
Race-day execution is elite, but Qualifying keeps leaving points on the table — and it's nearly all the door welcome. Goodbye and How-Are-You are consistently strong, but Hello has scored 65%, 25%, 25% and then 0% on 1 Jun. Because the weekend grid is handicapped by Qualifying, fixing the welcome is the single cheapest way to turn those P2 races into P1s. One coaching focus: a named greeter on the door at peak.
vs the chains — queue average (lower is better)
Attleborough out-runs the chains most weeks. Its 6-week average queue (~191s) already beats Nero (254s) and Costa (345s), and on its best week (147s) it's well clear — while the chains score 0% on Hello/Goodbye/How-Are-You. The hospitality moat is real; the one crack to seal is the Hello miss noted above.
